1. Overview

This manual presents an overview and high level user guide for accessing, understanding and navigating our eBest IOT Connected Detail of Dashboard. This will be a useful reference for a range of audience such as

a)      Executive management - to gain overall understanding of various metrics

b)      Sales Supervisors – To understand performance of their respective sales reps

c)      Territory managers – to understand how the individual outlets under their span is performing

The DocConverter in addition to providing a rich UI interface with many different views/screens catering to a large audience group have a lot of advanced functionality including ability to drill down or narrow down using a wide range of filters, flexibility to configure different parameters to different settings.

The DocConverter are organized into the following sections below.

  • Dashboards
  • Detailed View
  • Visit Report

 

Dashboards

This set of views/screens will provide an aggregated view. There are 7 different screens catering to different set of metrics and audiences.

  • Cooler Performance
  • Sales Correlation
  • Alarms
  • Alerts
  • Cooler Telemetry
  • Trade Channel
  • Customer tier

Each of these will be explained in the following sections.

Detailed View

This set of views will provide the ability to look at a more granular view and have different tabs that cover the following views.

  • Assets
  • Outlets
  • Alarms
  • Visit
  • Vision

More detailed info about these are provided in the relevant sections below.

Visit Report

This section provides a detailed report view of the different outlet visits that have been performed by the sales rep with ability to apply various filters to narrow down the results.

Cooler Performance Index

 

  • Cooler Performance Index: It is calculated as a weighted average of the following KPI’s

Weight is divided like this:

  • Door Opens Avg Daily (Weight = 40%). Calculate the percentage by dividing Door Opens Avg Daily by 48 (High target for door open close)
  • DocConverter Downloaded (Weight = 24%). Calculate percentage by dividing data downloaded by 100
  • Hours Power ON Avg Daily (Weight = 12%). Calculate percentage by dividing Hours power ON avg daily by 24
  • DocConverter Temp Avg Daily (Weight = 12%). Calculate percentage by diving Right temp average daily by 24
  • Hours Lights ON (Weight = 12%). Calculate percentage by diving Hours light ON by 24

Formulas 

  • Door Opens Avg Daily: Number of Door opens per day
    • Formula: For each DCPtrial take sum of total door counts divide by number of days with atleast one door open divided by unique asset count.
  • Data Downloaded- Number of assets which have health, movement or door records (Note: Location confirmation can also be showcased from the customers SFA solution)
    • Formula: Percentage of count of dcptrial assets from health record, door and movement divide by total assets.
  • Hours Power ON Avg Daily- Sum of all individual durations when the power was ON during the selected time period divided by selected time period (in days) divided by no of unique coolers.
  • Formula:  Total count of power records divided by count of unique assets for the selected duration.
  • Right Temp Avg Daily- Count of assets with right DocConverter per day (Temperature range <1 or >12)
    • Formula: Sum of hours with right temperature divide by number of days when data was received divide by count of unique assets
  • Hours Lights ON – Number of Hours light is ON per day.
    • Formula: Sum of hours when light was low, medium or high (exclude No light) divide by days when data was received divide by count of unique assets.

 

Last Data Downloaded:

          This always show data download respective to the period selected. For eg: If you Select Period as December 2016, and today is Feb 15 2017, then < 30 days will represent coolers that have data downloaded from Jan 15 th through Feb 15 th. It will work the same way for other criteria

          The visualization will show in % the count of Coolers distributed by the date when their data was downloaded.

  • Legend
    • Black will show coolers for which data was not downloaded in 90 days
    • Red would show coolers for which data was downloaded 60 days back or Before
    • Yellow will show coolers for which data was downloaded 30 days back or before
    • Green will show coolers with data downloaded within the last 30 days.

Formula:

  • Calculate the total count of assets for data <30 days
  • Calculate the total count of assets for data >30 and <60 days
  • Calculate the total count of assets for data >60 and <90 days
  • Calculate the total count of assets for data >90 days

 

 

Door Swings vs Target:

This pie chart rates Door Open close daily average against that target. User can see how many coolers of what     capacity have low door open close. The inner circle shows the % of coolers that are High, Low, medium. Door Open counts between +- 15% of target for that capacity is considered to be medium. Below medium is considered low and above medium is considered high. If you hoover over any of the pie it will show cooler count for the pie. Each inner pie (High, Medium, Low door counts) is further broken down by cooler capacity.

Door Target (DT): The target is defined by Asset Type which are assigned to the asset and summed by outlet(location). This field is part of master data

Actual Target (AT): The door target per outlet * Number of Days

Formula: Calculate the Sum of Door Target(DT) and Door Count(DC) by Outlet. Compare DT and DC with AT and categories outlets as Low, Medium and High using the below logic. This will form the inner dial of the chart

Low: DC< AT – (AT * 15%)

Medium: DC>=AT – (AT*15%) & DC<AT+(AT* 15%)

High: DC >=AT + (AT * 15%)

DocConverter categorize all the outlets under low, medium and high to the specific number of doors for that outlet. This will form the outer dial of the chart

Number of doors for the outlet can be calculated by additional of all the doors for the assets in a particular outlet.

 

 

Data Downloaded:  
 

This pie charts classifies the coolers based on data download during a given time period or range

 

Formula:

  • Data Downloaded: Percentage of count of unique assets from health record, door and movement divide by total assets.
  • Data Not Downloaded: Count of assets for which data is not downloaded.

  • Formula:
    • Low Light- Sum of total hours with low light and no light divided by number of days when data is available.
    • High Temperature-Sum of total hours with high temperature dcptrial divided by selected days when data is available
    • Power Off- Sum of hours with power OFF records divided by selected days when data is available.

2.3           Cooler Telemetry

This view offers details around how the analysis from the telemetry data that is received by the CDE and covers the elements below.

Cooler Health

  • Cooler Above 7C – Number of Coolers reporting at least one data point above 7 degrees during the selected time period
    • Formula : Count of Unique Coolers which has temperature above 7 degrees during the selected time period
  • Cooler with Low light - Number of coolers which have had a light status of ‘low light’ or ‘no light’ reading at least once during the time period under consideration
    • Formula : Count of Coolers reporting at least one data point below configured light status as No Light or Low Light during the selected time period
  • Hours Power off – Number of hours in a day the CDE was powered off (on Average)
    • Formula : Sum of all individual power off durations during the selected time period divided by selected time period (in days)  divided by  number of unique coolers
  • Low Utilization Coolers - Number of coolers with average door open count <10 per day
    • Formula : Count of number of coolers with average door open count  less than 10  per day or 70 per week or 300 per month
  • Missing Coolers - Number of coolers that are reported under missing status as observed during the sales rep visit or lack of any data received from asset for more than 30 days.
    • Formula : Count of coolers that are reported missing  during the selected time period 

Light Status (SmartTag, SmartVision)

Shows split of number of assets by different bands of light status as well as the asset percentage

  • Formula: Take count of asset and median of the light reading for that asset under that time period and distinguish according to light status classification


 

Power Off

Shows split of dcptrial of assets by different bands of ‘hours power off’ as well as the asset percentage.

  • Formula:  Create pivot table of the data with “Asset Serial” and sum “Power Off” (in hours). Based on this number, bucket into appropriate band as shown in x axis. Also, show the same split as percentage of asset.

 

 

Evaporator Fan

Shows split of number of assets by different bands of “hours Fan on” as well as the asset percentage.

  • Formula: Sum of duration for assets with machine activity as fan Type, bucket as per respective hours group

 

 

 

 

 

Compressor

DocConverter split of number of assets by different bands of “hours Compressor on” as well as the asset percentage.

  • Formula: Sum of duration for assets with machine activity of compressor Type, bucket as per respective hours group

 

 

Temperature

Shows split of number of assets by different bands of temperature as well as the asset percentage

  • Formula: Shows split of number of assets by temperature reading, take each applicable asset and average of the temp readings for that asset under that time period. Based on this number, bucket into appropriate band as shown in x axis. Also show the same split as percentage of asset.

Door Open

Shows split of number of assets by different bands based on the door opens as well as the asset percentage. Asset Count is displaying using the Bar graph & the percentage is displaying with the line graph

  • Formula: Shows split of number of assets by door open count. Take each applicable asset and count of all door opens for the selected time period. Based on the number, bucket into appropriate band as shown in x axis. Also show the same split as percentage of asset.

 

Temperature and Light Status

A pie chart showing split of asset count across those having temperature and light issues. For purposes of this graph, an asset is considered as having an issue with temp if it has at least one reading which is outside the normal operating range of 1-12 degrees. Similarly, an asset with at least one reading of low light or no light is taken into count for light malfunction.

  • Formula:

Temperature Issue: Find all assets which have Light intensity as “Medium or Full brightness” and Temperature greater than 12 and less than 1.

Temperature and Light Issue: Find all assets which have Light intensity as “No Light or Low Light” and Temperature greater than 12 and less than 1.

Temperature and Light OK: Find all assets which have Light intensity as DCPtrial or Full brightness” and Temperature greater than 1 and less than 12.

Light Malfunction: Find all assets which have Light intensity as “No Light or Low Light” and Temperature greater than 1 and less than 12.

Light Malfunction: Light intensity = “No Light or Low Light” and Temperature < 12 and Temperature > 1 and then Count unique assets

    • Formula: DCPtrial of unique assets with at least one alarm raised in selected time divided by total number of coolers) *100

  • Sales (Case Volume/Pieces)- Total number of sales against each customer tier.

  • Trend – DCPtrial the trending of the above sales metric as compared to the volume of sales with respect to the previous volume of sales order.(for example if current period is 1 st march to 15 th march then previous period would be 14 th feb to 28 feb i.e time period is calculated day wise , current period is 15 days and previous period id 15 days)
